Overview

Job Tracker is a self-hosted job search management system that automates the repetitive parts of job hunting. It scrapes listings from configured sources, scores each job against your resume and skills, lets you track applications through the full hiring pipeline, and monitors your inbox for employer replies.

What it does automatically
  • Scrapes job boards on a configurable schedule
  • Scores every job 0–100 against your skills
  • Detects duplicate listings across sources
  • Monitors email for recruiter responses
  • Sends follow-up reminders
What you do
  • Configure sources and your skill profile
  • Review high-scoring matches each day
  • Apply and log the outcome
  • Track status (phone screen → interview → offer)
  • Confirm email-to-job matches

Email Response

The Email Response page (/email-response) is the manual review hub for incoming employer emails. It sits between Applications and Analytics in the sidebar and shows a N yellow badge with the count of emails still awaiting a match.

Job Tracker automatically fetches emails from your configured IMAP accounts, classifies them (Declined or Interview), and tries to link each one to the correct job application using domain matching and keyword analysis. When the automation is confident it records the match immediately. When it is not — for example, if the reply came from a generic address like Gmail — the email lands here for you to match by hand.

Manual Match Workflow

Pairing an email to a job takes four steps:

  1. Click a job card on the left — it highlights with a blue border and checkmark.
  2. Click an email card on the right — it highlights blue.
  3. The button appears between the two panels.
  4. Click Match Selected to confirm the pair.

When a match is confirmed, the following happens automatically:

  • The EmailReview record is marked confirmed and linked to the selected application.
  • The application status is updated — a Declined email moves the status to Rejected; an Interview email advances a Submitted application to Phone Screen.
  • A Notification is created so the event appears in your Notifications feed.
  • The sender's domain is saved to the learning database (EmailMatchFeedback), improving future auto-matching accuracy.
  • Both cards are removed from the bottom panels and the new pair appears in the Confirmed Matches table at the top.
Why this page exists: The automated matcher works well when the email comes from a company domain (e.g. @acmecorp.com). It struggles with generic senders like Gmail or third-party ATS platforms that obscure the company domain. This page gives you a fast way to correct those gaps without touching any settings.
Tips:
  • Emails are fetched by clicking Check Now on the Settings page, or automatically when the scheduler daemon runs.
  • The Auto-suggested label shows the system's best guess — you are free to ignore it and select any job from the left panel instead.
  • After confirming a match the application status is updated immediately — check the Applications page to see the new status reflected there.

Settings

Settings stores your personal profile and controls how the app behaves. Changes take effect immediately — no restart needed.

Your Profile

  • Name, Email, Phone — Used to pre-fill application forms and cover letters.
  • Location & Miles — Sets the default job search radius for location-based scrapers.
  • LinkedIn & GitHub URLs — Included in application templates.
  • Resume Path — Absolute path to your resume file, used for skill extraction and cover letter AI.

Skills Configuration

  • Required Skills — Skills that must appear in a job description to score well. Missing required skills significantly lower the score.
  • Preferred Skills — Nice-to-have skills that boost the score if present.
  • Resume Skills — Skills extracted from your resume (auto-populated, or edit manually).
  • Minimum Match Score — Jobs below this score are excluded from scan notifications and the Recent Matches list.

Email Settings

  • IMAP / SMTP settings — Configure your email account so the tracker can monitor your inbox for job responses.
  • Check interval — How often the scheduler checks for new emails.

Scheduler (Daemon)

Start or stop the background scheduler from the Daemon section in Settings. See Scheduler Daemon for full details.

Other Settings

  • Follow-up Days — Default number of days after applying before a follow-up reminder is triggered.
  • Timezone — Used for scan scheduling and date display.
  • AI Cover Letters — Toggle AI-assisted cover letter generation on or off.

HTTPS & Secure Connections

By default Job Tracker serves over plain http://127.0.0.1:5000, which causes modern browsers (Chrome, Edge) to show a "Not Secure" warning. You can enable HTTPS using mkcert — a zero-configuration tool that generates locally-trusted certificates and installs its CA into the Windows, Chrome, and Firefox trust stores.

HTTPS here applies to local loopback traffic only (127.0.0.1). No data leaves your machine — this removes the browser warning and encrypts traffic between your browser and the local Flask server.

Once certificate files exist at certs\localhost.pem and certs\localhost-key.pem inside the Job Tracker folder, run.py detects them automatically and starts Flask with HTTPS enabled. The browser will open at https://127.0.0.1:5000 showing a padlock icon.

Step 1 — Install mkcert

Install mkcert once per developer machine. Open PowerShell and run:

PowerShell — install via Winget (recommended)
winget install FiloSottile.mkcert

Alternatively, download the latest mkcert-v*-windows-amd64.exe from github.com/FiloSottile/mkcert/releases, rename it to mkcert.exe, and place it on your PATH.

Then run the one-time CA registration in an Administrator PowerShell:

PowerShell (run as Administrator)
mkcert -install

This creates a private CA in %LOCALAPPDATA%\mkcert\ and registers it as trusted. You only need to do this once per machine.

Step 2 — Generate Certificates

From the Job Tracker root folder, run:

PowerShell — from the job_tracker/ folder
mkdir certs
cd certs
mkcert 127.0.0.1 localhost

Two files are created:

  • certs\localhost.pem — the certificate
  • certs\localhost-key.pem — the private key
The certs/ folder is listed in .gitignore. Never commit your private key to version control.

Restart Job Tracker (python run.py) and navigate to https://127.0.0.1:5000. Chrome should show a padlock with no warnings.

Certificate Renewal

Certificates expire after approximately 2 years. When they expire, the browser shows a certificate error. Regenerate them with:

PowerShell — from job_tracker/certs/
del localhost.pem localhost-key.pem
mkcert 127.0.0.1 localhost

Restart the app after regenerating. No other configuration is needed.

Check a certificate's expiry date with:
openssl x509 -in certs\localhost.pem -noout -dates

Scheduler Daemon

The Scheduler Daemon is a background process that runs scans and email checks on a configurable schedule — without you needing to manually trigger them. It runs independently of the browser.

Starting and stopping the daemon

Go to Settings → Scheduler to control the daemon:

Daemon control in Settings
Scheduler Daemon Running (PID 4821)
Scans every 6 hours · Email check every 30 min
  • Start Daemon — Launches the background scheduler process. The badge turns green and shows the PID.
  • Stop Daemon — Terminates the scheduler. Scans will not run until you start it again.
  • The daemon writes a scheduler_daemon.pid file so the app can detect if it is still running after a system restart.
Start the daemon once after setting up Job Tracker and leave it running. It will handle scans and email monitoring automatically in the background.

Tips & Tricks

Getting the most from scoring

  • Add all your technical skills to Required Skills and Preferred Skills in Settings — the more specific, the better the matching.
  • Keep your Minimum Match Score between 50 and 65 to balance volume with quality.
  • If you are getting too few results, lower the minimum score. If you are overwhelmed, raise it.

Staying organised

  • Set a Follow-up Date every time you apply. A week with no response is a good time to send a brief, polite email.
  • Use the Notes field on applications to record anything said on a call, questions you want to ask, or your impressions of the company.
  • Add interview times to your calendar using the Add to Calendar button — it works with Google, Outlook, and Apple Calendar.

Managing sources

  • Disable sources that are producing mostly low-score results — this speeds up scans and reduces noise.
  • Add very specific search URLs (e.g., python developer remote senior) rather than broad ones. Quality beats quantity.
  • Run a single source with the button after adding it to verify it works before your next scheduled scan.

Email integration

  • Configure IMAP credentials in Settings for automated email monitoring — the system classifies responses as Interview Invite, Rejection, Offer, etc.
  • Always review Email Response cards on the Dashboard — the auto-matcher occasionally needs correction, and accurate data improves your analytics.
Daily workflow: Open the Dashboard each morning, review new matches in the Recent Matches table, check Follow-ups Due, confirm any email reviews, then spend 20–30 minutes applying to your top matches.
